# RallyTech Tone Rules

## English

- Use launch-ready corporate language.
- Prefer direct verbs such as `plan`, `deliver`, `support`, `coordinate`, `integrate`, and `validate`.
- Avoid internal process phrases such as `draft layer`, `reference alignment`, or `for later editing`.
- Avoid exaggerated brand language unless explicitly requested.

## Traditional Chinese

- Use Taiwan B2B website tone.
- Prefer `協助`, `支援`, `整合`, `規劃`, `交付`, `驗證`, `維運`, `導入`.
- Avoid overly conversational wording.
- Keep the subject stable; do not switch between too many voices.

## Trust Signals

Good signals:

- delivery coordination
- commissioning readiness
- maintainability
- plant-data visibility
- cross-team alignment
- safer and more stable operations

Avoid unless verified:

- exact project counts
- exact founding year
- certification claims
- customer-name drops
- uptime or ROI promises
